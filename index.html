The Dissolution of Russian Consonant Clusters in the Crimean Tatar Language

Project by Binh Nguyen, 2022 REEES Think Tank Program

Mentors: Dr. Laada Bilaniuk (University of Washington), Dr. Darya Kavitskaya (University of California at Berkeley)

Crimean Tatar is a language spoken mostly in Eastern Europe and Central Asia by the Crimean Tatars, an ethnic group indigenous to the Crimean peninsula. Classified as a Turkic language, Crimean Tatar has around half a million native speakers today, and is deemed by UNESCO to be severely endangered. In this paper, we will look into the consonant clusters of Russian loanwords in Crimean Tatar to examine how the language has handled the different phonological structures of these borrowings.
